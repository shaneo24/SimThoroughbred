Help / Guide

Overview

SimThoroughbred is a horse racing stable management game set in Southern California. You start with $100,000 and build a stable by buying horses at auction, entering them in races, and collecting winnings, with the goal of breeding a stable of champions.

The Calendar

The game runs in weeks. Advance one week at a time using the Advance One Week button on the Dashboard. Each week costs $1,000–$1,500 per horse in training fees and $300–$450 per horse in vet fees.

The racing circuit follows a real-world Southern California schedule, switching between Santa Anita Park and Del Mar (more racing circuits will be added as the game advances through Beta).

Horses age on January 1 (week 1) each year, matching the universal thoroughbred birthday rule.

Buying Horses (Auction)

Eight horses are available at auction each week. You can see each horse's potential rating (Low / Fair / Elite) — but this rating has a chance of being wrong. Speed is intentionally hidden; you'll learn it through racing.

2-year-olds first appear at auction around April 1 (week 13). They're cheaper but unproven. 3-year-olds are partially developed and ready to race sooner.

After purchasing a horse you're given the option to rename them.

Horse Stats

  • Speed (hidden): Raw top speed. The primary factor in race results. Shown as a tier (Low / Fair / Elite) on cards.
  • Stamina (hidden): Determines ideal race distance. Running too far from ideal will worsen results.
  • Potential (hidden): How much a horse can improve over time. Higher potential means more room to develop.
  • Confidence: Horses with high confidence will perform better after winning.
  • Consistency: Horses with higher consistency will consistently run to their top ability.
  • Fatigue: Increases after racing. Recovers 10–15 pts per week. A horse with fatigue ≥ 70 cannot be entered. Any fatigue above 20 negatively impacts a horse's performance in a race.
  • Surface preference (hidden): Dirt, Turf, or Both. Running on the wrong surface worsens a horse's performance. Revealed through jockey notes after racing.

Horse Development

Horses under age 5 have an 11% chance per week of gaining +1 speed, up to their potential cap. 2-year-olds don't begin developing until May (week 18).

Horses aged 6+ may begin to slowly decline in speed over time.

Condition Book

The Condition Book lists all races for the current 2-week period at the active track. Each race has:

  • Race type — determines eligibility (see Race Types below)
  • Distance & Surface — match to your horse's stamina and surface preference
  • Division — age and sex restrictions
  • Purse — prize money split among the top 5 finishers
  • Entry fee — paid when you enter
  • Claiming price — if present, your horse may be purchased by another owner after the race

Each horse can only be entered in one race per condition book period and must wait at least 2 weeks between races.

Use Preview Next → to see the upcoming book before it becomes active.

Race Types & Eligibility

  • Maiden: For horses that have never won a race. You should start here.
  • Maiden Claiming: Maiden race where your horse may be claimed at the listed price. You should run here if your horse cannot win a maiden race.
  • Allowance N1X: For horses that have never won a non-maiden, non-claiming race. After you win a maiden race, you should run here.
  • Allowance N2X: For horses with at most one allowance/stakes win. Has an optional $100k claiming tag.
  • Claiming: Open to all. Your horse may be purchased after the race for the listed claiming price. Only run in these races after you've tried your horse in allowance races. Then gradually move them into easier races.
  • Claiming N2L: Claiming race restricted to horses with 1 win or fewer. Run here after you win a maiden claiming race.
  • Stakes: Prestigious fixed annual events. No claiming. Open to all eligible ages/sexes.

Payout split: 54% / 18% / 9% / 6.3% / 2.7% for 1st through 5th place.

Race Simulation

Your horse's performance is determined by:

  1. Effective speed — base speed adjusted for fatigue, win streak, consistency, distance fit, and surface preference
  2. Race difficulty — set by the track and race type
  3. Randomness — there's always a chance of an upset in either direction

Injuries

Every race carries injury risk. Injured horses are sidelined for 4–12 weeks and cannot race. They still accrue weekly training and vet fees.

Jockey Notes

After each race your jockey may leave an observation about surface fit or distance fit. These are the primary way to learn a horse's hidden surface preference and ideal trip. Up to 10 notes are stored per horse, viewable in the horse's Details panel.

Stakes Book

The Stakes Book shows the full year-round schedule of graded and listed stakes races for the SoCal circuit. Races appear in the Condition Book automatically when their week arrives — use the Stakes Book to plan ahead. You can filter by grade, track, surface, and division.

Breeders' Cup

The Breeders' Cup runs every year at Keeneland on November 1–2 (week 44). All 14 races are Grade 1 with purses from $1M to $7M. Entry opens 2 weeks before the event. Navigate to the Breeders' Cup tab in the Condition Book or Stakes Book to view and enter races.

Triple Crown

The three American classics run each spring:

  • Kentucky Derby — Week 18, May 4, Churchill Downs, 10f dirt, $5M, 20-horse field. Qualification required: finish top 3 in the San Felipe Stakes or Santa Anita Derby.
  • Preakness Stakes — Week 20, May 18, Pimlico, 9.5f dirt, $2M.
  • Belmont Stakes — Week 23, Jun 8, Belmont Park, 12f dirt, $2M.

All are restricted to 3-year-olds. Entry opens 2 weeks before each race.

Breeding Farm

Any filly or mare in your stable can be retired to the breeding farm using the button on her stable card. Retirement is permanent.

Breeding window: mid-January through mid-June (weeks 3–24). Select a stallion and pay the stud fee to breed. There is a chance the mare does not conceive; if she fails to conceive the stud fee is refunded. If conception is successful, the foal is born 51 weeks later. If the birth fails, the stud fee is also refunded.

Foal stats are derived from the parents: speed is based on the mare's speed and the stallion's speed. Surface preference has a chance of matching the sire. Other stats are randomly generated.

Foals appear under their dam's card on the Breeding tab. They turn age 1 on January 1, and age 2 the following January 1 — at which point you're prompted to name them and they enter your racing stable.

Mares are eventually pensioned from breeding due to health, between ages 22 and 30.

Farm costs: $300–400/week per active mare, $200–300/week per foal.

Saving & Loading

The game auto-saves to your browser's local storage after every action. Use Save to download a .json file backup, and Load to restore from a file. Save files are cryptographically signed — modified files will be rejected.

⚠️ Clearing your browser data will erase the auto-save. Use manual saves for long-term backups.
